TL;DR

We introduce NEXIS, a principled iterative procedure over sufficient and interpretable pre-treatment representations to identify the causal characterization of treatment effect heterogeneity — directly answering "what if, or how, should we modify our treatment assignment policy?"

Contributions

  1. Causal and interpretable HTE identification — we formulate identifying the direct effect modifiers Wdir as a Markov-blanket discovery problem over a sufficient and principally aligned pre-treatment representation, closing the gap between expressivity and interpretability.
  2. NEXIS algorithm — a forward-backward procedure with provable recovery guarantees (Theorem 1), empirically validated on a semi-synthetic CelebA benchmark, with practical ablations covering test choice, multiple-testing correction, spectral-gap threshold ρ, and backward elimination.
  3. Two real-world deployments — we augment the YOP (Uganda) and LEAP 1000 (Ghana) anti-poverty programs with satellite imagery, surfacing novel environmental impact heterogeneity explanations previously unreachable from survey data alone.

Method

From Tokens to Policy — a four-step cycle

  1. Run a controlled experiment and observe that the treatment effect varies across units. The key question is: which pre-treatment features actually cause that variation — i.e., which are the direct effect modifiers Wdir?
  2. Represent complex measurements (satellite imagery, medical imaging, …) via a pretrained foundation model + Sparse Autoencoder (SAE), producing thousands of sparse, interpretable candidate neurons alongside any structured covariates. Representation Sufficiency (SAE reconstruction loss) and Principal Alignment (one neuron per latent modifier) are the two key desiderata.
  3. Identify the direct effect modifiers — NEXIS runs a forward-backward Markov-blanket search over the candidate neurons, testing conditional CATE-equivalence at each step with Bonferroni-gated p-values. Each selected neuron is automatically labelled by a VLM (Qwen2.5-VL-72B) using top/bottom activating patches.
  4. Update policy accordingly — the output is a causal and interpretable characterisation of who benefits most, directly actionable for program adaptation. Unlike proxies or common-cause modifiers, direct modifiers license counterfactual comparisons across bundled program configurations.

Experimental Power Paradox

Why marginal screening always fails

Paradox

In a learned SAE dictionary with m ≫ r atoms, almost every coordinate is marginally correlated with the CATE — not because it is a direct modifier, but because of representation entanglement. As experimental power grows (larger n or larger η), marginal screening converges to M* ≈ [m] rather than S*: it accumulates non-direct modifiers backed by genuinely significant evidence. More data makes the problem worse, not better.

Recovering S* requires conditional reasoning: testing each candidate against the null that it adds no information given what is already selected. This is the design principle behind NEXIS. The forward step grows the selection whenever a candidate carries residual heterogeneity; the backward step prunes any previously retained coordinate that has become redundant. Together they target the minimal and sufficient representation.

On real deployments, the practical impact is dramatic: a marginal screen on the YOP Uganda trial returns 71 / 45 "discoveries" per outcome; NEXIS returns 5 / 2.

CelebA Semi-Synthetic Benchmark

Controlled validation with known ground truth

We build a semi-synthetic RCT on CelebA with |S*| = 2 known direct modifiers (wearing a hat, wearing eyeglasses). Pre-treatment information is face images only. A SAE with m = 13,824 codes trained on SigLIP embeddings provides the candidate dictionary. We sweep over effect magnitude η and sample size n across 50 seeds, measuring Precision, Recall, and IoU of S* recovery.

Methods

Application 1 — Uganda

Youth Opportunities Program (YOP, 2008)

YOP awarded cash grants to self-organised youth groups randomised across 439 groups in 331 communities in post-conflict Northern Uganda, targeting productive economic capacity. We extend the trial with Landsat-7 multispectral imagery (2005–2007 composite), embedded via Prithvi-EO and summarised by a learned SAE dictionary (1,024 atoms). NEXIS selects from 170 candidate features: SAE atoms, spectral indices (NDVI, NDWI, …), and demographic covariates.

A prior satellite analysis by Jerzak et al. (2023) found preliminary heterogeneity signal using binary image clustering; NEXIS extends this to interpretable, conditionally independent direct modifiers.

Linguistic-group heterogeneity in skilled employment.

Interpretation. For skilled employment, the pattern is unified by outside-option logic (Roy 1951): the grant's pull into a new skilled trade is strongest where alternatives are weakest. Karamojong speakers (marginalized pastoralists, GATE +0.674) show the highest impact; Teso/Acholi communities already affording productive pathways exhibit dampened effects. Perennial rivers and vegetation heterogeneity enable subsistence fishing/farming alternatives. For business assets, high NDVI amplifies impact while structured commercial-scale agriculture dampens it, consistent with Crépon et al. (2013) crowding-out.

Application 2 — Ghana

LEAP 1000 (Livelihood Empowerment Against Poverty, 2015–2017)

LEAP 1000 provided quarterly cash transfers to extremely poor households with newborns across 2,331 households in 162 communities in Northern/Upper East Ghana (5 districts: East Mamprusi, Karaga, Yendi, Bongo, Garu-Tempane). The difference-in-differences ATE is +7.35 GH₵/month on adult-equivalent consumption expenditure. We pool Landsat-8 2015 imagery (Prithvi-EO embedded, 4,096-atom SAE) with 24 survey covariates and spectral indices: 155 total candidates for NEXIS.

Discovered direct modifiers

Key finding NEXIS identifies two satellite-derived direct modifiers — ephemeral waterways and closed-canopy forest — and no demographic modifiers. In waterway/forest-active communities the program effect is 6–8× the average. No survey covariate survives FWER; the residual heterogeneity is purely environmental.

Marginal screening vs. NEXIS: 144 marginal "discoveries" collapse to 2 causal ones.

Mechanism — complementarity. Water-adjacent smallholders can direct transfers into irrigation-season agriculture (de Janvry & Sadoulet 2006); forest-edge households layer cash onto non-timber livelihood strategies unavailable elsewhere. Absence of demographic discoveries is itself informative: LEAP 1000's strict targeting already homogenises the recipient population, leaving environmental factors as the sole residual heterogeneity drivers.
